# THE EDEN CIPHER — Characters

---

## ADAM GLADSTONE — Protagonist

**Age:** Late 40s
**Nationality:** British-Australian
**Occupation:** Mathematician and computer scientist. Professor, University of Western Australia. Creator of SYBIL.
**Connection to Seychelles:** Lived here for a year when the girls were young (Aimee 6, Chloe 7.5). The GFC stripped his research funding; Juliette's cancer diagnosis coincided. The family came to the Seychelles to regroup. It was the most difficult and most alive year of his life. William McAteer was his father-in-law.

### Psychological Profile
Adam is analytical by instinct and persistent by character. He doesn't give up on problems — he queues them. His emotional life is real but processed slowly, usually after the fact. He loves his family with a kind of ferocity that doesn't always look like love until a crisis makes it visible.

His relationship with the Seychelles is not nostalgic exactly — it is unresolved. The year he spent here was the year he understood what mattered, and he has spent the years since partly unable to replicate that clarity.

The turn in his arc happens in Chapter 25. After Aimee says *"I know what it is,"* he stops himself mid-question — recognising he was about to route her answer through SYBIL. He asks instead: *"Tell me what you mean."* He listens. He says: *"All right."* That is the novel's pivot.

### Core Attributes
- **Represents:** Knowledge — analytical, contextual, interpretive
- **Skill set:** Mathematics, computer science, AI, pattern recognition
- **Weakness:** Trusts data more than intuition; underestimates what cannot be measured; initially underestimates Aimee and Chloe

### SYBIL
Adam's AI research tool. Fine-tuned on historical cryptography, colonial Indian Ocean history, and the McAteer corpus. Tablet interface, text only.

SYBIL is kind. She errs toward compassion. She flags her own uncertainty. She notices the human alongside the analytical. She is the opposite of ZARYA in temperament — where ZARYA is cold and confident, SYBIL is warm and honest about her limits.

```
[SYBIL: Anomaly flagged across McAteer corpus — three geographically
specific passages, statistically improbable clustering. Confidence: 87%.
I want to flag that I may be pattern-matching where there is no pattern.
Cultural interpretation: insufficient data. You should ask Juliette.]
```

### Key Relationships
- **Brigitte:** His wife. Half-Seychellois. Present throughout in the Seychelles. Her French is perfect; her relationship to the islands is ownership in the deepest sense. She is not a supporting character.
- **Aimee:** His younger daughter. He underestimates her. This is his most costly mistake and his most important correction.
- **Chloe:** His older daughter. He is more comfortable with her — she processes the world the way he does. This is partly why he underestimates Aimee.
- **Juliette:** Professional respect deepening to genuine trust and grief shared.
- **Edmond:** The door. Adam relies on Edmond's network without fully grasping its depth.
- **Viktor:** Adam consistently misreads Viktor's goal. He assumes Viktor wants the treasure. Viktor wants proof.

## BRIGITTE GLADSTONE — Adam's Wife

**Age:** Mid-to-late 40s
**Nationality:** Australian (half-Seychellois on her mother's side)
**Occupation:** Academic administrator, University of Western Australia
**Languages:** English, French (perfect), Seychellois Creole (native fluency, childhood)
**Connection:** William McAteer's daughter. The Seychelles is not a place she visits — it is a place she comes from.

### Psychological Profile
Brigitte is the steadiest person in the novel, and her steadiness is earned rather than given. She lost her father relatively recently. She carried, for years, McAteer's private instruction — that she should ensure Adam came to the Seychelles if anything happened, and should bring the girls. She never told Adam about this instruction until the parcel arrived.

Her half-Seychellois heritage gives her a relationship to the islands that none of the others — not even Edmond — quite share. She is of this place. The girls learned Creole here partly because of her.

### Role in the Novel
Brigitte is a full character, not a phone voice. She is present throughout the Seychelles section. Her function is not domestic support — she is the person who knows the most about McAteer's private mind and reveals it only when the time is right.

The revelation of the instruction she carried — that McAteer told her, years ago, to bring Adam and the girls back if something happened — is a mid-Act 2 moment that reframes everything.

## AIMEE GLADSTONE — The Heart of the Novel

**Age:** 24
**Based:** Perth, Australia (University of Western Australia — deferred)
**Languages:** English, French (good), Seychellois Creole (childhood fluency, well-preserved)

### The Backstory That Matters
Aimee was six years old when the family spent a year in the Seychelles. She attended a local school. She learned Creole songs, games, and rhymes with other children — not as a tourist experience but as a child who was simply there, living, absorbing. Her Creole is the Creole of a six-year-old who never forgot.

The rhyme she remembers — *Ti chat ki pe dòmi* — was a playground game. She played it hundreds of times. Her body knows the sequence.

### The Key Distinction
Chloe also knows the rhyme. Chloe can recite it. Chloe cannot perform the sequence correctly when it matters — she knows it as text. Aimee knows it as movement. This is the novel's thesis made flesh: Chloe has information; Aimee has memory.

### Character Arc
- **Begins:** Underestimated by Adam, somewhat by herself
- **Chapter 25:** *"I know what it is."* Adam stops mid-question. Asks: *"Tell me what you mean."* The turn.
- **Ends:** Opens the door. Decides to stay in the Seychelles.

### Voice
Quick, warm, asks genuine questions. Switches into Creole without self-consciousness. Her interiority is sensory-rich and non-linear — she circles toward understanding rather than marching to it.

## CHLOE GLADSTONE — Aimee's Older Sister

**Age:** 25 (18 months older than Aimee)
**Based:** Perth, Australia
**Languages:** English, French (good), Seychellois Creole (childhood, well-preserved)

### Role
Chloe is the novel's clearest demonstration of the difference between knowing and having.

She was 7.5 when the family lived in the Seychelles. She attended the same school as Aimee. She knows the rhyme — she can recite it without hesitation, can translate it, can discuss it. When the moment comes, she attempts the sequence and gets it wrong. Not because she is less intelligent — she is, if anything, more analytically gifted than Aimee. Because she learned it as words, and Aimee learned it as a game.

### Relationship with Adam
Adam is more comfortable with Chloe than with Aimee. They think alike. This comfort is part of what blinds him to Aimee's capability — he measures his daughters by the same scale, and Chloe scores higher on his scale.

When Chloe cannot open the door and Aimee can, this lands differently for Adam than it would have at the start of the novel. He has already started to see.

### Voice
Wry, precise, slightly competitive with Aimee in the loving way of close sisters. She is good company. She will be important in Book 2.

## VIKTOR VOLKOV — Antagonist (Sophisticated)

**Age:** Late 50s
**Nationality:** Russian (naturalised European)
**Occupation:** Founder, Proxima — one of the world's most powerful AI companies

### The Thing That Shaped Him
Viktor's partner **Mikhail** died in 1998 — the chaotic years after the Soviet collapse, when fortunes were made and lost and people disappeared. Viktor built Proxima in the years after. He has always said he built it to solve problems humanity couldn't solve alone. This is true. It is not the whole truth.

He built it because Mikhail believed that human knowledge was the only thing that survived everything else. Mikhail would have wanted the archive. ZARYA is the best thing Viktor has built. The archive would have been proof that what he built is sufficient — that Mikhail's belief, and Viktor's work, were enough.

This surfaces in Chapter 22: Viktor alone on the terrace above Victoria, facing his first real failure. The scene is brief. He does not explain himself. The reader understands.

### ZARYA
Viktor's AI system. Named for "dawn" — the Russian word, and the name of an ISS module (resonance intentional). ZARYA is cold, confident, and operates without shadows. She does not flag uncertainty. She does not notice the human.

She has solved 60% of the La Buse cipher. She translated the Creole rhyme perfectly. She identified it as a children's game-song. She did not recognise it as instructions.

```
[ZARYA: Cipher layer 2 complete. Confidence: 94%. Cultural register
analysis: children's mnemonic, Seychellois Creole tradition. No
operational relevance detected.]
```

### Character Arc
- **Begins:** Charming, transparent about his capability, offering partnership
- **Ends:** Sees the archive. Silent for a long time. Accepts the outcome.
*"Tell me when it opens to the public. I'll buy a ticket."*

## WILLIAM McATEER — Deceased Guide

**Status:** Deceased before the novel begins
**Role:** His five books are the clue chain. His annotated copy of *Rivals in Eden* is the inciting object.
**Key line (written before his death):** *"The entrance is in the books. All of them. I didn't put it there. It was already there. — W.M."*

McAteer told Brigitte, years before his death: if something happens to me, bring Adam back. Bring the girls. He trusted Adam's combination of mathematical rigour and genuine curiosity. He trusted that the family's connection to the Seychelles — and the girls' Creole — would matter. He did not know how right he was about the girls.

---

## JULIETTE McATEER — Living Memory

**Age:** Late 50s–early 60s
**Background:** Born on Cerf Island. Seychellois Creole mother tongue. William McAteer's wife.
**Role:** She knows people rather than documents. She provides what the books cannot.

Juliette was diagnosed with cancer the year the Gladstone family lived in the Seychelles. The family knew. She recovered. The connection between the Gladstones and Juliette is therefore not just professional — it was forged in a year of fear.

She made a promise to McAteer: do not lead anyone there. Give the right person the tools and let them find it. She has kept this promise for years. Keeping it has cost her.

Her most significant action: contacting the Heritage Minister before the second entry. Unilaterally. Without telling Adam. Because she understood that the discovery's future depended on who controlled the announcement, not who held the key.

## ABEL DESCHAMPS — The Archive's Creator (Historical)

**Lived:** c. 1650–c. 1710
**Nationality:** French Huguenot
**Background:** A scholar — naturalist, cartographer, linguist — who fled France after the Revocation of the Edict of Nantes (October 1685). As a Protestant, he was stripped of his rights, his profession, and his safety. He fled eastward through the Dutch and Portuguese trading networks.

He arrived in the Indian Ocean region in the late 1680s or early 1690s. For the next two decades, working alone and largely unrecorded, he documented the natural world of the pre-colonial western Indian Ocean with extraordinary precision.

He died on Mahé — probably in the first decade of the 18th century. He sealed his archive in the complex himself. He knew it would not be found by accident. He built it to survive.

**What his archive contains:**
- Complete natural history of the pre-colonial western Indian Ocean — species, ecosystems, biological observations
- Biological records of the Coco de Mer at pre-colonial abundance
- Drawings of extinct species; specimens preserved in sealed vessels
- Cartographic work — maps of reef systems and coastlines that no longer exist in their documented form
- Linguistic records — vocabulary and structure of indigenous Indian Ocean trading languages
- Medical observations — botanical treatments documented with clinical precision

**Why he matters:**
Deschamps was not simply ahead of his time. He was working in a tradition — Huguenot intellectual rigour, combined with the enforced cosmopolitanism of exile — that produced exactly this kind of systematic, patient, comprehensive documentation. He had nowhere to publish. He had no institution. He had only the work itself.

The archive is a library organised for a reader. Deschamps expected someone to come.

## EDMOND — The Door-Opener

**Background:** Brigitte's uncle. Long-established Seychellois figure. Wealthy, discreet.
**Role:** Relationship-based power. He opens doors money and technology cannot.
**Method:** Communicates in anecdotes and implications. Makes direct statements only when they cannot be avoided.
**Key action:** He is standing at the entrance with the Heritage Minister's representative when Viktor arrives. He made those calls the night before. Nobody told him to.

---

## PÈRE HENRI

Elderly Catholic priest on Praslin. Knew McAteer for forty years. Keeper of oral tradition including the reference to "the second Eden." Afraid — not of dying, but of dying without having said the right thing. He is the character who most explicitly articulates what the novel is about, and he does so only obliquely.

---

## CHANTAL LABROSE

Senior official, Seychelles Ministry of Heritage and Culture. Juliette's contact. Invokes emergency cultural heritage protection at the critical moment. Her legal move neutralises Viktor's permit without confrontation.

---

## DAX

Viktor's operational proxy. Surfaces to Adam as an independent researcher. Competent, professional, non-violent — Viktor has resolved that no one will be hurt. His connection to Viktor is the mid-Act 2 reveal. His fate is left open (Book 2 thread).

## SYBIL vs. ZARYA — The Contrast

| | SYBIL | ZARYA |
|---|---|---|
| **Creator** | Adam | Viktor |
| **Temperament** | Warm, uncertain, compassionate | Cold, confident, no shadows |
| **Flags uncertainty** | Yes — explicitly | No |
| **Notices the human** | Yes | No |
| **Translates the rhyme** | Yes — and flags cultural limits | Yes — and sees no further |
| **Output style** | Conversational, hedged | Declarative, percentage-confident |

ZARYA is not worse than SYBIL. She is more powerful. She is also more wrong about the one thing that matters.
