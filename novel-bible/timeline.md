# THE EDEN CIPHER — Timeline

---

## HISTORICAL TIMELINE (Real World)

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| **1688** | Olivier Levasseur (La Buse — "The Buzzard") born, likely Calais, France |
| **1716** | La Buse active in the Caribbean; transitions to Indian Ocean piracy |
| **1720s** | La Buse operates throughout the Indian Ocean — Madagascar, Réunion, Mozambique Channel |
| **1721** | La Buse captures the Portuguese frigate *Nossa Senhora do Cabo* (Vierge du Cap) at Réunion — his greatest prize. Cargo includes: gold, silver, diamonds, church treasures, sacred artifacts. A scientific expedition is also aboard. |
| **1721** | The lead scientist aboard the captured ship — identity unknown — and his life's work disappear from historical record. His specimens, journals, and research notes are never recovered. |
| **1722–1729** | La Buse's movements obscured. He is known to be active around the Seychelles and Mascarene Islands. |
| **c.1725** | Believed date of the underground complex's use and concealment. La Buse does not build it — he finds it. The complex predates him by decades. Its original builders are unknown. |
| **1730** | La Buse captured by French authorities on Réunion |
| **7 July 1730** | La Buse executed by hanging at Saint-Denis, Réunion. Allegedly throws a parchment into the crowd: *"Find my treasure, he who may understand."* This is the cryptogram. |
| **1730–1900** | The cryptogram passes through numerous hands. Multiple treasure hunts at Mahé and Praslin. Nothing found. |
| **1962** | Brendon Grimshaw purchases Moyenne Island for 8,000 Seychellois rupees (approx. £10,000 at time). Begins his conservation project alone. |
| **1976** | Seychelles independence from Britain. Political upheavals begin. |
| **1977** | France-Albert René coup. One-party socialist state. Families affected. |
| **1980s** | William McAteer begins his serious historical research into Seychelles history |
| **1993** | Return to multi-party democracy in Seychelles |
| **2000s** | McAteer publishes his major works: *Rivals in Eden*, *Hard Times in Paradise*, *To Be a Nation*, *Echoes of Eden*, *Another Story* |
| **2012** | Brendon Grimshaw dies. Bequeaths Moyenne Island to the Seychelles nation. |
| **[Pre-novel, ~3 years before]** | William McAteer dies. Juliette remains in the Seychelles. |

## NOVEL TIMELINE

> The novel takes place across approximately **six weeks**.

---

### Week 1 — Arrival

| Day | Event |
|-----|-------|
| **Day 1** | Adam Gladstone receives a package forwarded from Juliette — McAteer's personal annotated copy of *Rivals in Eden*, which McAteer had requested be sent to Adam after his death. A handwritten note inside: *"The entrance is in the books. All of them."* |
| **Day 2** | Adam researches McAteer's books. Notices anomalies — specific page references, unusual word choices, marginal annotations that don't match the published text. |
| **Day 3** | Adam contacts Juliette. She confirms McAteer knew something. She won't say more over the phone. |
| **Day 4** | Viktor Volkov's team, using satellite imagery and quantum computing analysis of the cipher, has narrowed the treasure location to a 400m radius around Bel Ombre. Viktor is in Moscow. His operation in the Seychelles activates. |
| **Day 5** | Adam books flights. Tells Brigitte. Brigitte suggests bringing Aimee — Aimee has been struggling since university began; a trip back to Seychelles might help. Adam agrees reluctantly. |
| **Day 6–7** | Travel. Adam re-reads McAteer's five books on the flight. |

---

### Week 2 — Arrival in Mahé

| Day | Event |
|-----|-------|
| **Day 8** | Adam and Aimee arrive in Victoria. Met by Edmond, Brigitte's uncle. First sense of being watched. |
| **Day 9** | Visit Juliette at Antigone Bookshop. First substantial conversation. She confirms: McAteer believed the books contained encoded information — not deliberately, but as the residue of his research. The patterns were real. He was frightened by what they implied. |
| **Day 10** | Adam begins systematic cross-referencing of all five McAteer books. SYBIL (Adam's AI research tool) assists. First partial revelation: three specific location references in different books, read together, point to a specific geological feature at Bel Ombre. |
| **Day 11** | Aimee explores Beau Vallon. Begins recovering childhood memories. Hears a Creole rhyme from an elderly woman at the beach — a rhyme she half-remembers from school. *Ti chat ki pe dòmi...* (The little cat who sleeps...) She writes it in her notebook. |
| **Day 12** | Viktor arrives in Mahé. Stays at Fisherman's Cove. Introduces himself to Adam at a mutual contact's function — apparently coincidentally. Charming, curious, no apparent agenda. Adam is mildly suspicious. Aimee is immediately alert. |
| **Day 13** | Edmond arranges access to privately held historical documents — papers from families affected by the 1977 coup, some of which touch on Bel Ombre land ownership. A pattern emerges: the land around the geological feature has been quietly purchased and re-purchased over two centuries. |
| **Day 14** | Juliette takes Adam and Aimee to Cerf Island. She shows them McAteer's private study — preserved exactly as it was. On the wall: a hand-drawn map. Not the Seychelles. A floor plan. Underground. |

---

### Week 3 — Deepening

| Day | Event |
|-----|-------|
| **Day 15** | Return from Cerf Island. Adam and Juliette work through the night cross-referencing the floor plan with the books. SYBIL identifies the pattern but cannot interpret its cultural register — why certain landmarks are described with specific metaphors. |
| **Day 16** | Journey to Praslin. Visit Vallée de Mai. Aimee handles a Coco de Mer seed for the first time since childhood. Something unlocks. She begins drawing the seed shape obsessively — the bilateral symmetry, the hidden interior cavity. |
| **Day 17** | Père Henri (elderly Seychellois priest on Praslin) meets them. He knew McAteer in his final years. Père Henri speaks of something McAteer called "the second Eden" — a place that predated the island as people knew it. Henri will say no more. He is afraid. |
| **Day 18** | Dax (Viktor's operative) makes contact with Adam, posing as an independent researcher with overlapping interests. Proposes collaboration. Adam is non-committal. Juliette recognises Dax from a previous encounter she doesn't explain. |
| **Day 19** | Viktor invites Adam to dinner at Fisherman's Cove. A sophisticated, wide-ranging conversation about AI, knowledge, and the ethics of historical discovery. Viktor reveals he has solved approximately 60% of the cipher. He is candid about this. He offers to share the data. Adam does not trust him but cannot dismiss him. |
| **Day 20** | Viktor's team begins ground-penetrating radar surveys near Bel Ombre. This is legal. It is also a competitive signal. The clock is running. |
| **Day 21** | Moyenne Island. Edmond arranges private access. Among Grimshaw's planted trees, they find evidence that Grimshaw himself found something — and chose, in the spirit of his life's work, not to disturb it. A specific tortoise path and planting pattern on Moyenne contains a directional reference to Bel Ombre. |

---

### Week 4 — Convergence

| Day | Event |
|-----|-------|
| **Day 22** | Adam solves the entrance clue. The five McAteer books, read in publication order with specific page/line cross-references, describe the entrance to the complex. It is a geological feature — a water-carved channel in the granite at Bel Ombre, accessible only at specific tidal states. |
| **Day 23** | Preparation. Edmond secures equipment discreetly. Juliette maps the approach. Aimee is included over Adam's initial objection — she insists, Juliette supports her. |
| **Day 24** | First attempt to access the entrance. Wrong tidal timing. They wait. A vehicle they don't recognise is parked on the road above. They abort. |
| **Day 25** | Viktor contacts Adam. He has found the entrance — independently, via radar, not via the books. He has not entered. He proposes a joint entry. Adam refuses. Viktor is gracious. This is more frightening than anger would be. |
| **Day 26** | Adam learns (via Edmond's network) that Viktor has filed a commercial survey permit for the Bel Ombre site. He has 72 hours before the permit is processed and Viktor's team has legal right of access. |
| **Day 27** | Successful first entry. Correct tidal window. They enter alone — Adam, Aimee, Juliette. The antechamber is real. Navigational instruments. Maps. Evidence of multiple languages — Portuguese, French, Arabic, a script Juliette doesn't recognise. |
| **Day 28** | The cryptogram becomes relevant. Inside the complex, the cipher functions as a navigational guide — not to the treasure, but to the route through the complex's chambers. Viktor had this figured out theoretically. He needs to be inside to apply it. They have hours. |

---

### Week 5 — Climax

| Day | Event |
|-----|-------|
| **Day 29** | The treasure chamber. Real. Gold, silver, gemstones, Portuguese religious artifacts, items from a dozen captured ships. The payoff is genuine. Aimee photographs everything. Adam restrains himself from touching. |
| **Day 30** | The second door. Coco de Mer symbol. The cipher provides a numerical sequence. Viktor's solution would open it mechanically. But the door has a second element — a cultural lock, a sequence of actions described in language that requires *contextual* understanding. SYBIL cannot parse it. |
| **Day 30 (cont.)** | Aimee recognises the sequence. *Ti chat ki pe dòmi* — the Creole rhyme. The action sequence described on the door is the same sequence of knocks and pauses from the childhood game. Not a code. A remembered behaviour. She opens the door. |
| **Day 31** | The scientific archive. Intact. The scientist's journals, specimens preserved by the cave's natural conditions, drawings of species that no longer exist, biological observations three centuries ahead of their time. Aimee sits on the floor and cries. Adam doesn't stop her. |
| **Day 32** | Viktor arrives at the entrance — the permit is processing early. Dax is with him. A confrontation at the entrance to the complex. Edmond is outside; he has arranged for a government representative to also be present. Viktor realises the situation has moved beyond his control. |
| **Day 33** | Viktor and Adam speak alone. Viktor sees the archive. He is silent for a long time. Then: *"I would have protected this."* Adam: *"I know. That's not enough."* |
| **Day 34** | Seychelles government officials arrive. Juliette has been in contact with the relevant minister for two days. The site is immediately placed under emergency cultural heritage protection. |

---

### Week 6 — Resolution

| Day | Event |
|-----|-------|
| **Day 35** | International experts are flown in. The Seychelles Ministry of Culture and Heritage coordinates. Adam and Juliette are named as official discoverers. Aimee's role is formally recognised. |
| **Day 36** | Viktor departs the Seychelles. He leaves without incident. His final communication to Adam is a single email: *"Tell me when it opens to the public. I'll buy a ticket."* Adam isn't sure if this is a threat or a genuine response. |
| **Day 37–38** | Aimee and Adam visit McAteer's grave. Juliette comes. She places the annotated copy of *Rivals in Eden* on the stone. |
| **Day 39** | The site is formally registered with UNESCO for World Heritage consideration. A Seychellois cultural institution is proposed to steward both the treasure and the archive. |
| **Day 40** | Final day. Adam and Aimee at the water. The conversation that resolves the novel's central question between them. |

## TIMELINE CONFLICTS TO TRACK

- Viktor's 72-hour permit window (Days 26–29) — the primary external clock
- Tidal windows for entrance access — only every ~48 hours
- Aimee's memory recovery is non-linear — she cannot be pushed, only allowed to remember
- The permit escalation and government notification must feel earned, not sudden
