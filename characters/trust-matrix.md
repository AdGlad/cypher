## Trust Changes by Act

### Act 1 (Chapters 1–12) — Building the Team

**Adam → Juliette:** Begins at zero. After Chapter 8 (the bookshop), rises to *cautious professional trust* — she gave him something; he has not yet demonstrated he can use it rightly.

**Juliette → Adam:** Begins at zero (she has waited; she is cautious about the right reader). After Chapter 8: *conditional* — she is opening the first door. After Chapter 11 (floor plan): *moderate professional trust* — he did the work; he asked the right questions.

**Juliette → Aimee:** Begins at zero; rises to *immediate recognition* by end of Chapter 8. This is the fastest trust development in the novel. It is earned not through demonstration but through recognition — Juliette sees what Aimee carries.

**Adam → Edmond:** Begins at low-moderate. After Chapter 9 dinner: stalled — Adam is not yet hearing Edmond's stories as information. Remains stalled through Chapter 12.

**Adam → Viktor:** Rises slightly in Chapter 9 (professional respect, surface level) then locks there. Adam does not distrust Viktor — he misreads him. This is different from low trust.

**Aimee → Viktor:** Drops at Chapter 9. She distrusts him before Adam does, without data. She says so once. Adam doesn't absorb it.

---

### Act 2 (Chapters 13–27) — Complication and Revelation

**Adam → Juliette:** After Chapter 17 (Père Henri visit — she sees him listen properly): *genuine professional trust*. After Chapter 22 (disappeared academic): *trust with stakes* — she is giving him something that costs her.

**Juliette → Adam:** After Chapter 17: She believes he might be the right reader. After Chapter 22: She is telling him things that increase his risk, which means she trusts him to use them correctly.

**Adam → Edmond:** Chapter 16 (the wall story — Adam begins to hear it): *rises to moderate*. Chapter 23 (Edmond reveals permit intelligence — Adam understands the full scope of what Edmond has been doing): *rises to high*.

**Adam → Dax:** Chapter 7 (first meeting as "Daniel Voss"): *low-moderate friendly professional trust* — a collegial field researcher. Chapter 23 (reveal): *collapses entirely*. Then: recalibrates. Dax was not lying about the history. He was lying about his employer. The professional respect remains, warily.

**Aimee → Juliette:** Continues to deepen throughout Act 2. Chapter 24 (the water scene, no dialogue needed): *near-complete trust*.

**Adam → Aimee:** Increases episodically (Chapter 10: he notices the rhyme note without understanding it; Chapter 17: he sees her go still after Père Henri) but the framework doesn't shift. He loves her more; he trusts her knowledge at the same inadequate level.

---

### Act 3 (Chapters 28–40) — Resolution

**Adam → Juliette:** Chapter 28 (she tells him she went to the entrance alone): the silence after her confession is Adam's highest trust action so far. He says nothing. He waits. She closes her eyes. This is *complete trust*.

**Juliette → Adam:** Chapter 28: *complete trust* — the last layer of what she's been holding. Then, Chapter 31 (she contacts Chantal without telling him): she acts unilaterally from that trust, which is something only complete trust permits.

**Adam → Aimee:** Chapter 30: he steps back. This is not stated as trust — it is enacted. *Complete trust*, late and hard-won, in the exact moment it is needed.

**Viktor → Adam:** By Chapter 35: *professional acknowledgment* — Viktor recognises Adam solved the right problem. Not warm; accurate.

**Adam → Viktor:** By Chapter 35: *complex neutral* — Adam understands that Viktor was not entirely wrong about the archive's value. He does not trust Viktor; he respects him as an honest opponent.

## What Earns Trust (by character)

### What earns Juliette's trust
- Asking the right questions rather than the impressive ones
- Treating oral information as information, not as colour
- Sitting with uncertainty rather than resolving it prematurely
- Noticing what Aimee carries

### What earns Edmond's trust
- Listening to the end of a story before responding
- Being interested in the story, not just the instruction inside it
- Not asking for more than the situation requires
- Demonstrating patience with Seychellois time

### What earns Père Henri's trust
- Aimee's presence (he trusts her before he trusts Adam)
- Acknowledging what he is afraid of, not minimising it
- Being willing to sit with the oral tradition on its own terms

### What earns Adam's trust (to give to others)
- Demonstrated competence in the domain of the investigation
- Directness — he trusts people who tell him what they know
- Consistency between stated intention and behaviour
- Being right about things (this is his lowest-common-denominator criterion)

### What earns Aimee's trust (to give to others)
- Seeing her — actually looking at what she's doing rather than at what her presence means to the task
- Asking what she thinks rather than what she knows
- Being willing to not understand immediately

## Trust That Is Never Fully Earned

**Adam → Aimee's knowledge (as knowledge):** He reaches it at Chapter 30. He does not retrospectively reclassify all prior interactions. The trust is earned at the climax, not reconstructed across the novel. This is realistic and important.

**Juliette → Viktor:** Never. She has known about his network's prior contact with the disappeared academic. She is not implacably hostile — she is accurately cautious. Viktor's concession at Chapter 35 does not change this.

**Père Henri → Dax:** The visit left damage. Père Henri gave Dax information he should not have given. He lives with this. It is not repaired.

---

## The Central Trust Argument

The novel's trust architecture makes a specific argument:

The kind of trust that opens the inner chamber — Adam trusting Aimee at the second door — cannot be manufactured, cannot be accelerated, and cannot be replaced by any amount of mutual professional respect. It requires Adam to exhaust his own tools, stand in front of a closed door with the best solution he can produce, and recognise that it is not enough.

Viktor's operation involves people who trust each other efficiently and professionally (Viktor/Dax). It does not produce the specific configuration that Chapter 30 requires. AURORA and Dax are excellent at what they do. What they do is not what the inner chamber needs.
