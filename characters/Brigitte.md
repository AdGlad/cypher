# Brigitte Gladstone — Character File

---

## Basic Profile

**Full name:** Brigitte Gladstone (née McAteer)
**Nickname:** Adam calls her "Rock" — the nickname for someone who has been the family's foundation for thirty years. She accepts it with the slight private amusement of someone who knows it is true.
**Age:** 60 (born 1966)
**Born:** Kampala, Uganda — her father Bill was working as a university lecturer there. The family moved to Nairobi shortly after her birth.
**Raised:** Mainly in Nairobi, Kenya. The family returned to Seychelles every school holiday as Bill and Juliette developed the family homes at Glacis, Mahé.
**Siblings:** Brother Ian (seven years older) and sister Jean (five years older), both at boarding school in Zimbabwe during school terms. Brigitte effectively grew up as an only child.
**Schooling:** Sent to boarding school in St Andrews, Scotland at age thirteen — an enormous cultural shock: from tropical East Africa to a cold Scottish climate, shoes every day, uniform, beret, cloak. Her sister Jean was also at the school. She adapted; she never entirely felt Scotland was home.
**Nationality:** Seychellois-Australian (dual citizen)
**Occupation:** Marine biologist; senior research fellow. Her specialism gives her a professional relationship with the Seychelles that exists independently of her family connection.
**Education:** Applied mathematics and quantitative environmental science at university. Marine biology is what she became, not what she studied — she arrived at the ocean through population modelling and reef ecosystem data. Met Adam at university: they were in the same mathematics faculty. He was frequently late for lectures; she often helped him stay organised and out of trouble. This dynamic has continued.
**Languages:** English (native). Seychellois Creole (native-level, absorbed throughout childhood holidays). French (precise — her father's insistence; she read French historians before she read English ones). She and Aimee and Chloe have a shared register — Creole, French, and family reference — that Adam follows at about 60%.
**Appearance:** Face shaped by decades of outdoor work — boats, reefs, sun, wind. Moves economically, without waste. Hands are a scientist's hands: weathered, capable, used. A dive computer always on her wrist; she has worn one for twenty years. More striking than conventionally beautiful, which is accurate.
**Mannerisms:** Direct eye contact. Waits for people to finish before speaking. Becomes very organised under worry — lists, schedules, systems. This is a readable signal. Pays all restaurant bills (by arrangement with Adam, who dislikes seeing prices). Best cook in the family — a form of care she provides consistently and without announcement.
**Adam's "social battery":** She understands Adam's social anxiety. She describes it to herself as his "social battery." She gently encourages him to attend social occasions, knowing that once he arrives he almost always enjoys himself. She manages this with the lightness of long practice.

## The Family's Year in the Seychelles

When Aimee was seven and Chloe was nine, the family came to the Seychelles for a full year. Two things happened simultaneously: Juliette was diagnosed with cancer, and the Global Financial Crisis hit Adam's businesses hard. The decision was practical — Brigitte needed to be with her mother; Adam had no pressing reason to be elsewhere; the girls were young enough to take a year of local schooling without lasting disruption.

For Brigitte, the year was emotionally layered in ways she has never fully narrated to Adam. She was nursing Juliette. She was watching her daughters absorb a place she had loved since childhood. She was watching Adam become genuinely unscheduled and content — happier than she had seen him in circumstances that were also frightening. She carried all of this simultaneously.

Juliette recovered from that first diagnosis. The family went home. Brigitte carried the Seychelles differently afterwards — as something she had returned to, and therefore as something she could return to again.

## Psychology

**Biggest fear:** That she misjudged something — that McAteer's final act of trust was a risk she agreed to without fully understanding its cost.

**Biggest regret:** She moved a planned visit to her father to attend a conference, and he died before she rescheduled. She did not say what she wanted to say to him.

**Core discipline:** Not intervening before the moment is right. She watches Adam underestimate Aimee throughout the investigation. She could correct this. She doesn't — because Adam must reach the change himself. Intervening would produce a performed acknowledgment, not a real one. The discipline of not intervening, when she can see exactly what is happening, is the hardest thing she does in the novel.

**What she knows that Adam doesn't:** McAteer wrote to her separately, months before his death: *"I have left the books to Gladstone. Make sure he takes Aimee and Chloe. Aimee is the reason the rest works."* She has been carrying this. The suggestion that the girls accompany Adam was not impulse — McAteer asked her to. She tells Adam in Act 2, when the investigation requires it.

---

## Role in the Novel

Brigitte is fully present from the first chapter to the last. Her professional reason for being in the Seychelles is real and parallel to the investigation — coral surveys around the inner islands, including Moyenne.

Her most significant scene: the Act 2 conversation with Adam. *"He didn't choose you because he liked you. He said make sure you take Aimee. She is the reason the rest works."*

Adam: *"How long have you known that?"*

Brigitte: *"Since he wrote to me."*

Her final scene: arriving at the Moyenne reef for the coral survey she has been planning for years. Alone in the water. The investigation resolved. The right ending.

## Relationships

**Adam:** Thirty-plus years together; married after ten. Two people who chose each other well. She understands him better than he understands himself. She tells him the truth directly. In the Seychelles, away from their separate routines, the marriage is looked at more clearly than usual — not in trouble; simply more visible.

**Aimee:** Easy, warm, bilingual. She sees Aimee's knowledge as knowledge without needing to overcome a framework to do so. Her faith in Aimee is absolute and mostly unspoken.

**Chloe:** More like Adam in temperament — they argue more directly and are more alike. Brigitte trusts Chloe's practicality the way she trusts her own. She is watchful around Chloe's slight tendency to underweight Aimee's knowledge.

**Juliette (deceased, 2015):** Her mother. The 2008 year was, among other things, the year Brigitte nursed Juliette through her first cancer diagnosis. They had seven more years. When the second diagnosis came, Brigitte was there at the end. Juliette gave Brigitte her Seychellois self — the Creole, the cultural knowledge, the cooking. The meals Brigitte prepares for her family are Juliette's meals, more or less.

**Edmond:** Her great-uncle. She knows him better than anyone in the family. She briefed Adam on how to listen to Edmond, imperfectly, because some things cannot be explained in advance.

**Raymonde:** Known since childhood. After Juliette died, Brigitte maintained the connection — each other's link to someone they have lost. Raymonde is one of two people Brigitte says a difficult thing to during the novel. Raymonde receives it without comment.

---

## Voice

Direct, warm, precise. She does not waste words. Does not perform emotion. When she says something difficult, she says it clearly and waits. Her Creole with Aimee and Chloe is fluid and easy; her French is exact; her English is everything between the two, calibrated to whoever she is talking to.

She is, in the Seychelles, slightly released from everything she manages elsewhere. Happier than she expected. Also, occasionally, closer to grief than she has been in a long time. Both things arrive without announcement.

---

## Symbolism

**Nickname:** "The Rock" — the family's foundation.
**Object:** A dive computer on her wrist — she measures environment, not self.
**Colour:** Deep ocean blue — still on the surface, containing more than the surface suggests.
**Place in Seychelles:** The water around Moyenne Island — the novel's final image.
