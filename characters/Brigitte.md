# Brigitte Gladstone — Character File

---

## Basic Profile

**Full name:** Brigitte Anne Gladstone (née McAteer)
**Age:** 44
**Nationality:** Seychellois-Australian (born Seychelles; moved to Australia in her late teens; dual citizen)
**Occupation:** Marine biologist; senior research fellow, [Australian university — TBD]. Her work focuses on Indian Ocean coral ecosystems. Her specialism gives her a professional relationship with the Seychelles that exists entirely independently of her family connection — she would be here anyway.
**Education:** BSc Marine Biology, University of Western Australia. PhD Marine Ecology, same institution — where she met Adam at a faculty event he almost didn't attend.
**Languages:** English (native). Seychellois Creole (native-level — her mother tongue before English). French (perfect — her father's insistence; she read French historians before she read anyone else). She speaks Creole with Aimee and Chloe privately. She and the girls have a language built from Creole, French, and references Adam doesn't share.
**Appearance:** The appearance of someone whose face has been shaped by outdoor work — she has spent decades in boats and on reefs. Darker than she was at twenty. Moves economically, without waste. Her hands are a marine biologist's hands — weathered, capable. She is often described by people who know her as more striking than conventionally beautiful, which is accurate.
**Mannerisms:** Direct eye contact. Waits for people to finish before speaking. Does three things simultaneously and does all three well. When something worries her she becomes very organised — schedules, lists, systems. She is the family's logistical intelligence.
**Health:** Excellent. Physically active; she does not sit still easily.
**Strengths:** Practical intelligence. Emotional clarity — she knows what she feels and knows when to act on it. The capacity to see Adam, Aimee, and Chloe each clearly and love all three without taking sides. Professional competence that is entirely separate from the family investigation and gives her an independence no one fully accounts for.
**Weaknesses:** Can be impatient with people who take longer to reach conclusions she reached an hour ago. Has McAteer's tendency to process things privately and share only when she's ready. Sometimes her practicality means she solves the wrong version of a problem. She has been carrying McAteer's private instruction about Aimee for years and has not fully examined what it cost her to do so.
**Skills:** Ocean navigation, diving, marine surveying. The same ability to read landscape that Aimee has — but for underwater terrain. Reads Seychellois social situations faster than Adam. The best cook in the family, which she treats as a minor skill and is wrong about.

## The Family's Year in the Seychelles

When Aimee was six and Chloe was seven and a half, the family came to the Seychelles for a year. Two things happened simultaneously: Juliette was diagnosed with cancer, and the Global Financial Crisis stripped Adam's research funding away almost overnight. The decision was practical — Brigitte needed to be with her mother; Adam had no immediate reason to be elsewhere; the girls were young enough to take a year of local schooling without lasting disruption.

In practice, it was the most formative year of Aimee's and Chloe's lives. The school. The language. The landscape. The friendships. Juliette recovered. Adam found himself genuinely free for the first time in his adult life — no deliverables, no seminars, just reading and thinking. He was, by his own later admission, happier than he had any right to be given the circumstances.

The family returned to Australia at the year's end. The girls carried the Seychelles back with them in different ways. Brigitte had always carried it.

## Psychology

**Biggest fear:** That she misjudged something — that McAteer's final act of trust was also a risk she agreed to without fully understanding, and that she has spent years managing the consequences of a promise she made before she knew what it would require.

**Biggest regret:** She was not in the Seychelles when McAteer died. She had planned to visit that spring. She moved the trip once for a conference, and then he was gone. She and Adam share this particular unfinished business, though they arrived at it from different directions.

**Core belief:** That the Seychelles is real in a way other places are not — that what her father documented, and what Grimshaw conserved, and what Juliette carries, matters in a way that should be taken seriously by serious people.

**Internal conflict:** She is present throughout the investigation, which means she watches Adam underestimate Aimee in real time. She knows better. She also knows that intervening will not change Adam's framework — he has to reach the change himself. Not intervening, when she can see exactly what's happening, is the hardest thing she does in the novel.

**What she knows that Adam doesn't:** McAteer wrote to her separately, months before his death: *"I have left the books to Gladstone. Make sure he takes Aimee and Chloe. Aimee is the reason the rest works."* She has been carrying this. The suggestion that the girls accompany Adam was not impulse — McAteer asked her to. She has not told Adam this.

## Role in the Novel

Brigitte is fully present from the first chapter to the last — she travels to the Seychelles with Adam, Aimee, and Chloe, and she stays. Her professional reason for being there (the coral surveys she has been planning for years around the inner islands, including Moyenne) is real and parallel to the investigation.

She is not a co-investigator — she works her own research while the investigation proceeds. But she is the family's anchor. She knows Juliette better than Adam does. She reads Edmond faster than Adam does. She understands the Seychelles in a way that is different from Aimee's embodied knowledge and from Adam's analytical approach — it is the knowledge of someone who grew up between two worlds and learned to navigate both.

Her most significant act in the novel: the conversation she has with Adam in Act 2, when she tells him — for the first time — what McAteer actually said. *"He didn't choose you because he liked you. He said make sure you take Aimee. She is the reason the rest works."* Adam says nothing for a long time. Then: *"How long have you known that?"* Brigitte: *"Since he wrote to me."*

Her arrival at Moyenne Island in the final chapter — for the coral survey she's been planning for years — is the novel's last image. The right ending.

## Relationships

**Adam:** Eighteen years of genuine marriage — two people who chose each other well and who have both been absorbed in their respective work. She understands him better than he understands himself. She tells him the truth directly, which he depends on even when he doesn't want it. Being in the Seychelles together, away from their separate professional routines, surfaces things that have been quietly unattended. The marriage is not in trouble; it is simply being looked at more clearly than usual.

**Aimee:** Easy, warm, bilingual. She sees Aimee's knowledge as knowledge — she doesn't need to overcome a framework to do this. Her faith in Aimee is absolute and mostly unspoken. Aimee knows it. Watching Adam not see what Brigitte sees is the most sustained frustration of the novel for her.

**Chloe:** Different from her relationship with Aimee — Chloe is more like Adam in temperament, which Brigitte finds both familiar and occasionally maddening. Chloe and Brigitte argue more directly; they are also more alike. Brigitte trusts Chloe's practicality the way she trusts her own.

**Juliette:** Her mother. Their reunion after McAteer's death is not shown on the page — it happened before the novel begins. What the reader sees is the aftermath: two women who have already done the hard part and are now functioning. Their love is present in how Brigitte handles Juliette's objects, how she stands in Juliette's kitchen, how she says nothing when Juliette needs a moment.

**Edmond:** Her great-uncle. She knows him better than anyone. She briefed Adam on how to listen to Edmond, imperfectly, because some things cannot be explained in advance.

---

## Voice

Direct, warm, precise. She does not waste words. She does not perform emotion. When she says something difficult, she says it clearly and waits. Her calls on Aimee and Chloe are the same — she notices what they're doing and names it without drama.

Her physical presence in the Seychelles brings a different quality to the narrative than her off-stage phone-call version would have. She is someone slightly released from everything she manages elsewhere. Happier than she expected, in a year that began with grief.

---

## Symbolism

**Object:** A dive computer on her wrist — she has worn one for twenty years. Measures depth, time underwater, and ascent rate.
**Colour:** Deep ocean blue — still, containing more than the surface suggests.
**Place in Seychelles:** The water around Moyenne Island — where she conducts her coral survey, alone, in the novel's final scene.
