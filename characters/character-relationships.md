## Bilateral Relationship Notes

### Adam ↔ Aimee
**Dynamic:** Father and daughter whose love is real and whose epistemic relationship is the novel's central tension. Adam consistently assigns lower weight to Aimee's contributions than the situation warrants — not from contempt, but from a framework that has no category for embodied cultural knowledge.
**Arc:** From functional underestimation (he treats her as logistical support) to earned recognition (he steps back at the second door).
**Key moments:** Chapter 6 (he notices she's more herself here); Chapter 25 (she says "I know what it is" and he doesn't hear it properly); Chapter 27 (she says something quiet that keeps him awake); Chapter 30 (he steps back).
**Power dynamic:** Nominally Adam-led; actually equal by Chapter 25; Aimee-led at the climax.

### Adam ↔ Viktor
**Dynamic:** Intellectual equals in different domains who are running parallel investigations with different tools toward a different understanding of the same object. They respect each other. They are in genuine opposition.
**Arc:** Adam consistently underestimates Viktor's goals (he thinks Viktor wants treasure; Viktor wants the archive). When Adam understands what Viktor actually wants, the situation becomes more complicated, because Viktor is not entirely wrong about the archive's value.
**Key moments:** Chapter 9 (first meeting — Viktor is already fully informed); Chapter 22 (Adam discovers Viktor's AI flagged the same pattern, different conclusion); Chapter 35 (Viktor at the entrance — the legal situation resolves).
**The argument they represent:** Viktor = data + processing power. Adam = data + cultural context. Viktor's advantage is measurable. Adam's advantage is not.

### Adam ↔ Juliette
**Dynamic:** Carefully managed mutual assessment that becomes, by Chapter 28, genuine trust. Juliette gives trust in stages, calibrated to Adam's demonstrated readiness. Adam earns it through what he notices, not through what he argues.
**Arc:** Cautious respect → professional trust → personal trust. The moment of genuine trust is when Adam says nothing after Juliette tells him she went to the entrance alone. He waits. She closes her eyes briefly.
**Key moments:** Chapter 8 (first meeting — she gives him the books); Chapter 11 (the floor plan); Chapter 22 (she tells him about the disappeared academic); Chapter 28 (she tells him she went alone).

### Adam ↔ Edmond
**Dynamic:** Adam initially reads Edmond's anecdotes as social convention rather than information delivery. The moment he learns to listen to Edmond's stories as data is a turning point.
**Arc:** Polite courtesy → frustrated patience → genuine comprehension. By Chapter 23 Adam has learned to sit with Edmond's stories and wait for the instruction inside them.
**Key moments:** Chapter 9 (first meeting — Adam misses the first instruction); Chapter 16 (Edmond's land records story — Adam is starting to hear); Chapter 23 (Edmond tells Adam about Viktor's permits — Adam understands everything and asks the right question in response).

### Adam ↔ McAteer (deceased)
**Dynamic:** The most significant intellectual relationship of Adam's adult life that he never quite got right. He converses with McAteer through the annotated copy throughout the novel. His arc with McAteer is grief processed through a practical problem.
**The discovery:** Adam discovers during the novel that he was not chosen because McAteer liked him. He was chosen because McAteer knew him — knew exactly what he could do and exactly where his limits were. This understanding changes the assignment.
**Key moments:** Chapter 1 (receives the parcel); Chapter 22 (realises McAteer knew about the entrance for years); Chapter 38 (McAteer's grave — the last scene).

### Aimee ↔ Juliette
**Dynamic:** Immediate recognition. Both culturally bilingual; both comfortable with the Seychelles in a way Adam is not quite. Juliette sees what Aimee carries before anyone else does.
**Arc:** The fastest-developing relationship in the novel. By Chapter 15 they are having conversations Adam can follow only in outline.
**Key moments:** Chapter 8 (Juliette watches Aimee handle the annotated copy — Aimee opens it to the right page without being told); Chapter 14 (they discuss the coco de mer drawing; Juliette doesn't tell Aimee what it means — she waits); Chapter 17 (after Père Henri — they sit together on the water and neither speaks for a long time).

### Aimee ↔ Viktor
**Dynamic:** She distrusts him before Adam does, without data. She says so once, briefly, and doesn't labour it. Viktor does not model her as a relevant variable.
**The asymmetry:** Viktor's omission of Aimee from his strategic picture is his fatal miscalculation. He has assessed Adam (correctly), Juliette (cautiously), Edmond (incompletely). He has not assessed Aimee at all.

### Viktor ↔ Dax
**Dynamic:** Functional dependency; the most complete mutual understanding in the novel. Viktor gives Dax latitude no one else receives because Dax understands three levels of every instruction. Viktor is aware that this understanding is uncomfortable; he does not resolve this discomfort.
**The Aimee instruction:** Viktor gives it explicitly in Chapter 23, unprompted. Dax notes the specificity. This line holds.

### Juliette ↔ Brigitte
**Dynamic:** Mother and daughter in every practical sense. This relationship is mostly off-stage — Brigitte is largely absent from the Seychelles chapters. It is present in the texture of how Juliette talks about Brigitte and in the weight of Brigitte's arrival at the novel's end.

### Juliette ↔ Edmond
**Dynamic:** Siblings who understand each other completely and operate independently. They do not coordinate formally — they share a network and a worldview. When Juliette needs Edmond to act, she says so directly (uniquely — she is the one person to whom he is direct in return).

### Edmond ↔ Brigitte
**Dynamic:** Great-uncle and niece who are closer than that formal distance implies. Edmond was the adult presence in Brigitte's childhood. She knows his method. She briefed Adam, imperfectly, on how to listen to him.

### Underestimation — who underestimates whom
| Who underestimates | Who is underestimated | Cost |
|-------------------|----------------------|------|
| Adam | Aimee | Nearly opens wrong chamber |
| Viktor | Aimee | Does not assess her; loses the inner chamber |
| Viktor | Edmond | Does not map Seychellois social fabric; permit is delayed |
| Adam | Viktor's goals | Assumes treasure, misses archive motive; costs time |
