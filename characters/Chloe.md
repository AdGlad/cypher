# Chloe Gladstone — Character File

---

## Basic Profile

**Full name:** Chloe Juliette Gladstone
**Middle name:** Juliette — named after her grandmother Juliette McAteer
**Age:** 25 (born 1 January 2000 — two years and six months older than Aimee)
**Born:** Manly Hospital, Sydney, during the Millennium celebrations. Adam and Brigitte watched the New Year's Eve fireworks over Sydney Harbour through the hospital ward window.
**Nationality:** Australian (born Australia; Seychellois heritage through Brigitte)
**Occupation:** Registered Paediatric Nurse, based in Sydney's Northern Beaches. She genuinely loves caring for children and supporting families during difficult times.
**Education:** Nursing degree (Registered Nurse qualification).
**Languages:** English (native). Seychellois Creole (conversational — she was nine during the Seychelles year, old enough to acquire it well but not quite at the absorption age Aimee was at seven). French (functional). She knows *Ti chat ki pe dòmi* word for word — can recite every line. She cannot perform the sequence.
**Appearance:** Tall, athletic build, broad shoulders from years of swimming. Fair blonde hair, blue eyes. Casual but neat dresser — noticeably more organised than Aimee. **No tattoos** — she would never get one. This is occasionally mentioned in relation to Aimee's gecko.
**Mannerisms:** Asks small specific questions when she is processing something larger. When anxious she sits pulling individual hairs apart looking for split ends — a habit she is unaware of and which the family all recognise as her tell. Uses people's names naturally, a nursing habit.
**Health:** Excellent. Outstanding swimmer; won medals in Seychelles for swimming and water polo.
**Strengths:** Highly compassionate. Calm during emergencies — trained and practised. Exceptional memory for people (names, faces, conversations, tiny personal details). Naturally observant; reads behavioural changes instinctively. Dependable, loyal, emotionally mature.
**Weaknesses:** Overthinks decisions under time pressure. Overly cautious — prefers informed action to instinct. Carries enormous self-imposed responsibility. Finds it difficult to disappoint people. Sometimes neglects her own needs while attending to everyone else's.
**Skills:** Paediatric nursing (crisis management, patient assessment, precise communication under pressure). Exceptional memory for people — operationally useful in the investigation. Outstanding swimming. Conversational Creole.
**Habits:** The split ends habit (unconscious, tells the family she's worried before she says so). Cooks when she needs to decompress — methodical, generous. Quiet evenings with a film after busy shifts.
**Partner:** Callum (teacher) — long-term, stable, committed. Marriage a likely future step. He is not present in the Seychelles for this novel.
**Fears:** Making the wrong decision when someone depends on her. Disappointing her parents. Spiders and other creepy crawlies.

## The Seychelles — Annual Holidays and the 2008 Year

From early childhood, Chloe and Aimee travelled to the Seychelles every Australian winter (June–August) for up to six weeks, staying at Bill and Juliette's house in Glacis, northern Mahé. They went to Beau Vallon beach, explored the islands by boat (Amit's *Sea Shell*), and visited Cerf Island to see Edmond. Adam came sometimes; other times he remained in Australia for work. These annual visits gave Chloe and Aimee a childhood geography of Mahé that is embedded rather than visited.

**The 2008 year:** Chloe was nine during the family's full year in the Seychelles. She and Aimee attended the Independent School, Victoria, Mahé. Chloe's Creole came quickly — she was competitive about it, in the way older children are competitive, wanting to match her younger sister. She did match her, almost. But the gap between nine and seven is real: Aimee's Creole went in at the cellular level; Chloe's went in as a language she learned.

She remembers the year vividly and consciously. The school, the friends, the beach, the tortoises on Moyenne Island. She can tell you what happened; Aimee can show you what it felt like.

She also knows *Ti chat ki pe dòmi*. She learned it at school, sang it in the playground, knows every word. She could recite it now if asked.

She cannot perform it.

This is the distinction the novel is built around, and Chloe is its clearest illustration: knowing a thing is not the same as having it in your body. Chloe knows the rhyme. Aimee's body knows the sequence. When Aimee opens the second door, Chloe is standing beside her — knowing every word, and unable to do what her younger sister does.

**Bel Ombre:** She visited Bel Ombre with Adam and Aimee during childhood. She was less captivated by the treasure story than by the simple pleasure of being somewhere difficult and beautiful with her family. She remembers it as a family afternoon. Aimee remembers it as a moment of revelation.

## Psychology

**Biggest fear:** Making the wrong decision when someone depends on her. She worries about letting people down — her parents especially.

**Core belief:** That caring for people is the most important thing a person can do. She does not say this. She demonstrates it continuously.

**Internal conflict:** She carries responsibility as a natural shape — it is how she moves through the world — but she has built safety from it too. Risk means the possibility of being wrong; being wrong means letting someone down. The investigation asks her repeatedly to act without certainty.

**Her relationship with her own Creole knowledge:** She has never thought of it as insufficient. She has good Creole. She can do things with it. The moment at the second door — when Aimee's body does what Chloe's cannot — is when she finally understands the difference between knowing and having. She understands it immediately and completely, the way she understands a diagnosis.

## Relationships

**Aimee:** The defining relationship of both their lives. Extremely close; very different in personality. Chloe is cautious; Aimee is adventurous — constant good-natured sibling rivalry. Aimee borrows Chloe's clothes without asking, which irritates Chloe enormously. Chloe wishes Aimee would show more respect for her advice as the older sister. Despite all of this, Chloe would do absolutely anything to protect Aimee. She will be the first person to say, after the second door opens: *"You knew, didn't you. Since Beau Vallon."*

**Adam:** She deeply admires his intelligence and kindness. She also worries about him. Adam admires Chloe enormously and wishes she would take more risks. He cannot say no to her.

**Brigitte:** Brigitte is Chloe's role model. She has inherited much of Brigitte's sense of responsibility, compassion, and organisational instincts. Direct, occasionally combative, always honest — they are alike and know it.

**Juliette (deceased, 2015):** Warm and slightly formal — Chloe loved Juliette, though Juliette's knowledge was not Chloe's knowledge. She was respectful, attentive, honest. Juliette was fond of her and trusted her practically. The deeper recognition — the one Juliette had with Aimee — was not there, and neither of them minded.

**Viktor:** She notices him with the specificity of someone trained to read people under stress. She files what she distrusts without naming it.

## Role in the Novel

Chloe is present throughout. She is not the novel's central consciousness — that is Adam and Aimee — but she is the family's emotional anchor and its most reliable voice. She:

- Manages the human climate of the group — keeps people functional under pressure
- Her exceptional memory for people surfaces at key investigative moments: a face at the marina, a name from an early conversation, a detail from Edmond that connects something three chapters later
- Stays calm when others cannot — trained and practised
- Stands beside Aimee at the second door — knowing the rhyme, unable to perform it, watching her younger sister do the thing she cannot

Her ending is not as transformed as Aimee's. She returns to her life — Callum, the ward, the work she loves. But she returns having understood something she hadn't before: the difference between knowing and having, and the particular courage required to act without certainty.

## Dialogue

Warm, measured, precise. She answers before asking. Uses people's names — a nursing habit. Says *"I think"* before an opinion because she means it literally. In a crisis she becomes more precise, not less.

**Key lines:**
- *"I'm not saying don't. I'm saying tell me what we do if it goes wrong."*
- *"What do you need right now?"* — asked in any crisis, to any person. The nurse's reflex.
- After the second door opens: *"You knew, didn't you. Since Beau Vallon."*

Aimee: *"I thought I did."*

Chloe: *"You did."*

## Symbolism

**Object:** Her hands — still when stillness is needed, precise when action is required.
**Colour:** Sand — warm, useful, more present than it seems.
**No tattoos vs. Aimee's gecko:** Chloe does not mark herself impulsively with things she cannot undo. This is her character in one detail.
**Place in Seychelles:** The water itself — she is most herself moving through something.
