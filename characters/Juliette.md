# Juliette McAteer — Character File

---

## Status in the Novel

**Juliette McAteer died in 2015.** She does not appear in person. She is present throughout as a felt absence — in Edmond's memory, in Brigitte's grief, in the Creole she gave Aimee and Chloe, in the friendship with Raymonde that shaped everything, and in the promise she made to McAteer that she could not complete before she died.

The novel is set in 2026. She has been dead for eleven years. McAteer outlived her by several years. Her death changed his final period profoundly — he became more urgent, more private, more focused on ensuring his knowledge reached the right person.

---

## Basic Profile

**Full name:** Juliette Eugénie McAteer (née Beaumont)
**Died:** 2015 — cancer, almost certainly a recurrence of the diagnosis that brought the family to the Seychelles when Aimee was six. She recovered from the first diagnosis. She did not recover from the second.
**Nationality:** Seychellois
**Occupation:** Academic linguist. Held a lectureship at the University of Edinburgh during McAteer's appointments there. Later gave lectures at the University of Seychelles and the National Heritage Museum. Her DPhil thesis on Seychellois Creole oral tradition remains the most cited academic work on the oral literature of the Seychelles.
**Languages:** Seychellois Creole (mother tongue). English (fluent). French (fluent). Malgache (functional).

---

## What She Did (Before the Novel)

**Her thesis** documented forty-seven oral texts — songs, games, rhymes, stories — that existed nowhere in written form. Among them: *Ti chat ki pe dòmi.* She did not know, when she collected and transcribed it, that it would open a door. It is in the National Heritage Museum archive because of her. SYBIL finds it there.

**Her friendship with Raymonde** began when both were approximately eighteen. They were the closest of friends for nearly forty years. Bill McAteer met Juliette at Fisherman's Cove — Raymonde's hotel at Beau Vallon — at an evening social event. The friendship of the two women is the reason McAteer and Juliette met at all.

**Her promise to McAteer:** He told her what he believed about the books before he told anyone else. She was the keeper of his plan — the annotated copy, the instructions, the specific choice of Adam as recipient. When she died in 2015, she passed the obligation to Edmond. She had time to do this properly. She extracted from Edmond the same promise she had given McAteer: wait for the right moment; give the right person the tools; don't lead anyone there directly.

**Her visit to the entrance:** She went once, three years before her death, on a morning when she had the tidal charts and understood enough of the pattern. She reached the rock formation at the channel entrance. She stopped. She had given her word. She turned back. She told no one — not Edmond, not McAteer. Raymonde knows. Juliette told her the night before she went, in the way you tell your oldest friend something you haven't told anyone else.

## How She Is Present in the Novel

**In Edmond:** He carries her method alongside his own. The patience, the staged revelation, the trust in the right reader — these were hers before they were his. When he holds something back from Adam, he is honouring her judgment as much as his own.

**In Brigitte:** Brigitte lost her mother in 2015. The year the family spent in the Seychelles when Aimee was six — the cancer diagnosis that year, the recovery — and then the second diagnosis, and the death. Brigitte carries this without performing it. Adam sometimes forgets that being here is complicated for Brigitte in ways that have nothing to do with the investigation.

**In Aimee and Chloe:** Juliette is the reason the girls speak Creole. She spoke it with them whenever they visited, long before the year in the Seychelles. She is the source of the language that will open the door.

**In Raymonde:** Raymonde's eccentricity deepened after Juliette died. She was already moving toward her current life — forced out of Fisherman's Cove, retreating toward Bel Ombre — but Juliette's death settled something in her. She stopped pretending she was going back to anything. The house near Bel Ombre, the parrot, the bees, the garden: this is the life Raymonde built after losing her oldest friend.

**In the archive:** Juliette's thesis is the document that preserves *Ti chat ki pe dòmi* in writing. SYBIL finds it in the National Heritage Museum digital archive and flags it to Adam in Chapter 29. Juliette's academic work is the chain that connects the oral tradition to the modern investigation. She documented the key without knowing she had.
