## Prologue — 1727 and 1730

**Scene 1 (c.1727 — Bel Ombre, Mahé, before dawn)**
La Buse wades through the tidal channel in the granite at Bel Ombre. He has been here before. He retrieves cached treasure from the first chamber of an underground complex. Beyond a low stone arch: a second chamber with shelves cut into granite, hundreds of preserved volumes, glass cylinders sealed with wax, large drawings of plants and birds — everything numbered, everything ordered, in no language he reads. He does not touch it. He scratches a marker into the granite wall — a fragment that will not be understood for three hundred years — and leaves before the tide returns.

**Scene 2 (7 July 1730 — Saint-Denis, Réunion)**
La Buse on the gallows. He pulls a parchment from inside his coat and holds it up. *"Trouvez mon trésor, qui peut le comprendre."* He throws it into the crowd. One man catches it. The trap falls.

*Two hundred and ninety-nine years later.*

## Act One — The Parcel (Chapters 1–12)

**Ch 1 — Perth, Adam's office (UWA)**
A parcel from the Seychelles. Return address: Edmond Beaumont, Cerf Island. Inside: McAteer's personal annotated copy of *Rivals in Eden*. Note inside the back cover in McAteer's hand: *"The entrance is in the books. All of them. I didn't put it there. It was already there."* Adam photographs everything. Runs it through SYBIL. She will not finish until Chapter 4.

**Ch 2 — Perth, Adam's home**
Adam calls Edmond. Edmond confirms McAteer's instructions: Adam is the chosen reader. Will say nothing more on the phone. *"Come."* Adam books the flight. Who was McAteer — woven through as memory.

**Ch 3 — Nicosia, Viktor's office [VIKTOR POV]**
Viktor reviews the Seychelles operation. ZARYA has processed the La Buse cipher (60% resolved) and all five McAteer books. She has flagged the same statistical anomaly as SYBIL. Viktor has reached the wrong conclusion. He orders active phase. Requests information on recent academic interest in McAteer's books.

**Ch 4 — Perth, Adam's office (day before departure)**
SYBIL delivers full analysis: 14 geographical precision clusters across Books 1, 2, 4; anomalous footnote citation pattern; near-duplicate phrases 19 years apart. p < 0.02. She cannot interpret it — *"on-site correlation is the appropriate next step."* Adam packs the annotated book and the location map.

**Ch 5 — Perth, video call (evening before departure)**
Brigitte, Aimee, Chloe already in Seychelles (Brigitte's coral survey season; staying at Cerf Island). Family video call. Aimee asks sharp questions about Bel Ombre. Mentions she dreamed about it last week. Adam says: probably not useful data. He is wrong.

**Ch 6 — Arrival, Mahé**
Adam lands. Edmond meets him. In the car: context, land ownership history, forty years of watching Bel Ombre. Edmond embraces Aimee first.

**Ch 7 — Victoria, Day 1**
National Archives: someone has requested the same colonial land survey documents three days ago. Antigone Bookshop: owner remembers McAteer coming every week in his last year. *"Always the same pages of his own book."*

**Ch 8 — Antigone Bookshop, Day 2 [EDMOND replaces Juliette]**
Edmond waiting at the bookshop. Explains McAteer's belief: his research inadvertently mapped something. He did not publish the suspicion. He wanted the right reader to find it through the books themselves. *"He said the books must do the work. The reader must earn the knowing."*

**Ch 9 — Fisherman's Cove Marina, Day 3 [VIKTOR POV]**
Viktor arrives. Radar survey data: anomaly in the substrate at Bel Ombre. Natural cave formation — or not only that. Viktor requests information on recent UK arrivals. Adam Gladstone's name comes back within an hour.

**Ch 10 — Beau Vallon beach, Day 4**
Aimee alone at Beau Vallon. An elderly woman selling woven baskets mutters a fragment of the Creole rhyme. *Ti chat ki pe dòmi.* Aimee recognises it. Writes it in her notebook without knowing why.

**Ch 11 — Cerf Island, Day 7 [EDMOND's family home]**
Edmond takes them to Cerf Island. McAteer's study, preserved exactly. Under a cloth on the wall: a hand-drawn floor plan. Underground space, multiple chambers. Largest annotation: *The second half.* No entrance marked. The plan assumes you are already inside.

**Ch 12 — Victoria, evening Day 7**
Viktor's commercial survey permit for Bel Ombre filed — shell company OceanVector Ltd. Edmond identifies the beneficial owner: Volkov. The 72-hour clock begins. Adam commits: find the entrance before Viktor does. Edmond: *"Then we need to go to Praslin."*

## Act Two — The Investigation (Chapters 13–30)

**Ch 13–16 — Praslin**
Père Henri Delacroix: the oral tradition of "the room God made before He made the garden." Gives Adam a 1943 letter from a lay preacher describing "a passage cut by men who were not of this time" at northern Bel Ombre. Viktor's team is one step behind on Praslin.

**Ch 17 — Praslin, second visit**
Père Henri: *"The key to that room is not written down anywhere. It lives in the people."* Aimee hears this. Something shifts.

**Ch 18–21 — The cross-referencing work**
Adam, SYBIL, the five McAteer books. The fourteen passages. The tidal window. The specific orientation. The land ownership gap in the record at the northern Bel Ombre strip. The picture forms: a tidal channel in the granite, accessible only at a specific low tide in a specific orientation. Cruise-Wilkins dug for twenty years and found nothing because he was digging. The entrance is not dug — it is revealed.

**Ch 22 — Victoria [VIKTOR POV — midpoint]**
Viktor discovers Adam has correctly interpreted the McAteer anomaly while he hasn't. ZARYA had the same data and reached the wrong conclusion — she assumed the entrance would be obvious; the cipher would navigate from the surface. It doesn't. That night, Viktor alone on the roof terrace above the harbour. He looks at Moyenne Island. He thinks about Mikhail — partner, dead 1998, who believed in Proxima before it existed. ZARYA is the best thing he has built. ZARYA cannot solve this. Meanwhile: ZARYA has identified the Big Dig on Moyenne Island (Grimshaw's deliberately constructed double chamber) as the primary site. Correct data. Wrong conclusion.

**Ch 23 — Mahé**
Dax approaches Adam directly. Offers Viktor's partnership. Viktor's explicit instruction: Aimee is off-limits. Adam refuses the partnership.

**Ch 24 — Moyenne Island**
Brigitte's coral survey brings her to Moyenne. She sees the Big Dig. Reports to Adam: constructed, not natural. But on Moyenne, not Bel Ombre. Wrong island.

**Ch 25 — Mahé**
Aimee: *"I know what it is."* Adam starts to ask her to demonstrate. Stops himself — hears himself from the outside. Asks instead: *"Tell me what you mean."* She explains from sensation, not method. He listens without translating it into data. He says: *"All right."* THE TURN.

**Ch 26 — Near Bel Ombre — Raymonde's house**
Adam and Aimee visit Raymonde. The parrot. The dogs. The garden. She speaks French at Adam and Creole with Aimee. She takes them to the edge of her property where you can see the granite headland. She tells them she went into the channel once. Felt the air change. Came back. *"Juliette said someone would come."* She gives Aimee a jar of honey when they leave. She says something in Creole to Aimee that Adam doesn't catch.

**Ch 27–28 — The solution**
Adam solves the entrance from the fourteen passages and the 1943 letter. SYBIL confirms. They plan the approach — the tidal window, the timing. Edmond tells Adam something he has held for years (a second stage revelation — exact content TBD, related to McAteer's final private notes or the inner chamber).

**Ch 29 — Mahé**
SYBIL's most important output: *"This sequence is also structurally similar to a documented Seychellois children's song. I cannot determine if the similarity is coincidental. Aimee may know this song."* — Juliette's thesis, in the National Heritage Museum digital archive. Meanwhile: Raymonde, without telling Adam, calls Chantal Labrose at the Ministry of Heritage. Emergency cultural heritage protection invoked on Bel Ombre. Viktor's permit frozen.

**Ch 30 — Bel Ombre, before dawn**
The tidal window. Adam, Aimee, Chloe, Brigitte at the channel entrance. They enter. Layer 1 solved.

## Act Three — The Entrance (Chapters 31–40)

**Ch 31–33 — Inside the complex**
The first chamber: La Buse's cache. Gold, silver, sacred objects from the *Nossa Senhora do Cabo*, untouched for nearly three centuries. Viktor's cipher (the La Roncière cryptogram) navigates the interior passages. Adam works it. Viktor's 60% solution plus Adam's cultural interpretation.

**Ch 34 — Viktor's team arrives**
Forty minutes after Adam and Aimee enter. Edmond and Raymonde are at the entrance. The heritage protection is already filed. Viktor stands at the channel and assesses. He knows when a position is lost.

**Ch 35–36 — The inner chamber door**
Stone, sealed, no visible mechanism. Adam tries the cipher sequence. The door does not move. He looks at Aimee.

She says the first line of the rhyme: *Ti chat, ti chat ki pe dòmi.* Her body takes over. Three knocks on the cold stone to the left of the arch. Step left. Step right. Both hands flat on the central keystone. The mechanism releases. The door opens.

**Ch 37 — The archive**
Stillness. Sealed air. Shelves in granite. Glass vessels. Hundreds of bound volumes. Large drawings of species — some of which no longer exist anywhere on earth. Abel Deschamps's life's work. A record of the natural world of the Indian Ocean before European colonisation changed it permanently.

On the inner wall of the first chamber: La Buse's marker. Adam reads it with his lantern. The prologue is understood.

Viktor is brought in to see it. He is genuinely still. He says: *"Tell me when it opens to the public. I'll buy a ticket."* He means it.

**Ch 38 — Chloe and Aimee**
*"You knew, didn't you. Since Beau Vallon."*
Aimee: *"I knew. I just didn't know how I knew."*
Chloe: *"You did."*

**Ch 39 — Aftermath**
The legal and institutional consequences. Chantal's heritage protection holds. The Deschamps-McAteer Institute begins to take shape. Viktor leaves — through channels, through the academic process. He says so and means it.

**Ch 40 — Final image**
Aimee tells Adam she is not going back to Australia yet. Adam: *"I think Bill knew you'd say that."*

Brigitte in the water above the Moyenne reef — the coral survey she has been planning for years. Looking up through clear light.
