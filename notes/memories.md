## Author's Working Memory

### What the Book Is Really About
At its core, THE EDEN CIPHER is about the difference between what a machine can know and what a human can understand. Viktor's AI is more powerful than SYBIL. Viktor's AI is more powerful than Adam. Neither matters when the knowledge required is *embodied* — lived in the body, not stored in data.

Aimee opens the door not because she is cleverer than Viktor's system. She opens it because she played a game on a beach when she was eight years old and her muscles remember the sequence.

This should never be stated directly as a thesis. It should only ever be *shown*.

## Atmosphere Notes

- The Seychelles sun is heavy. It doesn't simply warm — it presses.
- The granite boulders of the Seychelles are unlike any other geological formation — they are rounded, vast, tactile, warm at midday
- The ocean around the inner islands is shallow and clear — you can see the bottom in fifteen feet of water
- Victoria smells of spice, diesel, and salt — in that order depending on the wind
- Night in the Seychelles is darker than most places; the stars are visible
