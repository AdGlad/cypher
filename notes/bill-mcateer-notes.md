# Notes — William McAteer (Working Notes)

---

## Why McAteer Works as a Character Device

McAteer is the novel's perfect absent guide because:

1. **He was meticulous.** His research was thorough enough that the patterns are genuinely there — not planted, but present as the natural residue of thorough documentation.

2. **He did not intend the clues.** This is crucial. If McAteer deliberately hid clues in his books, the novel becomes a different kind of story — a code-hidden-in-a-text story. Instead, the patterns emerge from the *density* of real historical research. The clue is that the truth was always there.

3. **He knew and chose not to act.** In his final years, McAteer noticed the pattern. He did not publish his suspicion, because he understood that how the knowledge was found would determine what happened to it. He trusted the right reader to find it themselves.

4. **His method is the novel's argument.** Patient, cumulative, thorough — McAteer's approach to history is the opposite of Viktor's AI approach. And yet McAteer's method created something Viktor's AI cannot fully process.

## McAteer's Relationship with the Seychelles

- Came to the Seychelles as an academic historian
- Spent decades embedded in the community
- Juliette is his wife — born on Cerf Island, deeply rooted in local knowledge
- His historical research was therefore informed by living sources as much as archival sources
- This combination — documented history + lived knowledge — is what makes his books more than data

## The Annotated Copy of Rivals in Eden

The copy Juliette sends to Adam is McAteer's personal working copy. His annotations:

- Some are normal scholarly notes — cross-references, corrections, queries
- A small number are different in character — circled phrases, unexplained symbols in the margins, a series of what appear to be page numbers from other books
- The final annotation, inside the back cover: *"The entrance is in the books. All of them. I didn't put it there. It was already there. — W.M."*

This note was written before McAteer's death and left with Juliette with specific instructions: it should be forwarded to Adam Gladstone after McAteer's death. He knew Adam by reputation through Brigitte's family connection.

## Key Line to Establish

In the novel, McAteer's private note must feel authentic — like something a meticulous historian would write. Not dramatic. Not performative. Just precise and weighted:

> *"The entrance is in the books. All of them. I didn't put it there. It was already there."*

This line carries the novel's central argument in miniature: the truth was always present; it required only the right attention to become visible.
