# René Antoine Lafortune — Historical Figure File

*For use in THE EDEN CIPHER. Lafortune died 2007. His family may be alive in the Seychelles. Handle with appropriate care.*

---

## 1. VERIFIED FACTS (PUBLIC RECORD)

- Seychellois man; son of a local fisherman
- Joined Grimshaw's project on Moyenne Island aged approximately 19 (late 1960s or early 1970s — exact date not documented)
- Remained for approximately 35–40 years
- Described by Grimshaw as "much more than a mere Man Friday" — the dedication in *Another Grain of Sand*
- *Another Grain of Sand* (2009) is subtitled "The Story of Two Men and an Island" — explicitly a tribute to him
- Died 2007, cerebral malaria, Singapore
- Burial location not documented in public sources
- Grimshaw lived alone on Moyenne Island for his remaining five years after Lafortune's death

**What his role was:** Partner in every practical aspect of island life — path-cutting, tree planting, tortoise care, hosting visitors. Not an employee in a conventional sense; a permanent co-inhabitant and collaborator.

## 2. NOVEL USAGE

### If Lafortune appears in the novel

Lafortune is dead before the novel begins (died 2007). He is a figure of island memory — people who visited Moyenne in the 1970s–2000s will have met him. He might appear in:

- Memory or anecdote when characters discuss Grimshaw and Moyenne
- Historical accounts (one of Grimshaw's book passages, paraphrased)
- A brief reference from Edmond or Juliette, who would have known or known of him as part of the Seychellois community

He should not be invented beyond what the public record establishes.

### The Grimshaw-Lafortune parallel for the novel's themes

The partnership is relevant to the novel's argument. Grimshaw (British, outsider, came with intention) and Lafortune (Seychellois, insider, came with knowledge of the place) together built something neither could have built alone. This is a small mirror of Adam and Aimee, or of McAteer and Juliette. It doesn't need to be stated. It can be present as texture.

## 3. LEGAL AND ETHICAL NOTES

Lafortune died in 2007. His family — children, siblings — may still be in the Seychelles. He was not a public figure in the same way as Grimshaw; he was documented because of his association with Grimshaw. He should be referenced with care:

- Safe: All documented facts from Grimshaw's books and public sources
- Use with care: Do not invent private views, inner life, or undocumented behaviour
- Do not create a speaking fictional version of Lafortune without careful consideration of his family's potential response

If the novel visits Moyenne Island in a scene set before 2007, Lafortune could appear as a minor background figure — described only through documented attributes. This should be handled briefly and respectfully.
