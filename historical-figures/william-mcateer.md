**Biography (confirmed)**

## 3. LEGAL AND ETHICAL NOTES

McAteer is a real person who died relatively recently. His family may be alive. The following is important:

**Safe to use:**
- All biographical facts from the public record
- His published books, their historical content, and his documented methodology
- The public figure's characteristics as derived from his published work (thoroughness, archival depth, etc.)
- Naming him as Adam's deceased father-in-law in the fictional world
- Quoting or paraphrasing passages from his real books (fair use for literary purposes)

**Use with care:**
- The fictional McAteer should not be depicted doing anything that contradicts known facts about the real man
- The fictional McAteer's inner life, private conversations, and undocumented views are the novel's invention — the author's note should acknowledge that the historical character is the inspiration for a fictional character, not a documentary portrait
- Nothing in the novel should harm the reputation of the real McAteer or his family

**The autobiographical dimension:** The fictional McAteer is Brigitte's father and Juliette's husband. These relationships are fictional. If the real McAteer had a real family, they should be handled with full discretion.

**Recommended author's note framing:**
> *The historian William McAteer wrote five books about the Seychelles that I have read and cherished. The William McAteer in this novel is a fictional character inspired by his work and by the kind of devoted attention to a place that his books represent. I have tried to honour the spirit of the real historian while giving the fictional one a story he never lived.*
