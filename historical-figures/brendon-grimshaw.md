## 1. VERIFIED FACTS

*(All facts in this section are confirmed across multiple independent sources. Full research: `/Users/adglad/git/cypher/research/brendon-grimshaw-research.md`)*

**Birth and Death**
- Born: 27 July 1925, Dewsbury, West Yorkshire, England
- Died: 3 July 2012, Victoria, Seychelles, aged 86
- Cause: "a short illness" — specifics not publicly documented
- Buried: On Moyenne Island, beside his father Raymond and two "Unhappily Unknown" pirate graves
- Tombstone: "Brendon D. Grimshaw 1925–2012. Moyenne taught him to open his eyes to the beauty around him and say thank you to God."

**Journalism Career**
- Batley News (West Yorkshire) — Britain's youngest chief reporter at approximately 23
- The Star, Sheffield — trained with Donald Trelford (later editor of *The Observer*)
- East African Standard, Nairobi — Senior Sub-editor, Features Editor; carried a shoulder-holster gun during Mau Mau period
- Tanganyika Standard and Sunday News, Dar es Salaam — Editor and director of both papers
- Tanzania's first PR consultant under President Julius Nyerere, 1970–1973

**Moyenne Island**
- Bought 1962 from Philippe Georges for £8,000 — "four minutes to midnight on the last day of his stay"
- Island at purchase: rat-infested, almost no birds, described as "just four trees" among dense overgrown scrub
- Did not move permanently until 1973 (Eduardo Mondlane assassination a catalyst for leaving Africa)
- Planted 16,000+ trees over five decades; 120 Aldabra giant tortoises; ~200 bird species; 4.8km trails
- 2009: Moyenne designated Moyenne Island National Park — "world's smallest national park"
- Refused all purchase offers including ~$50M from Saudi interests: "The island is not for sale."
- Will: island to be maintained for "prayer, peace, tranquillity, relaxation and knowledge"

**René Antoine Lafortune**
- Seychellois, son of a local fisherman; joined Grimshaw aged ~19 in the late 1960s or early 1970s
- Partnership lasted ~35–40 years; described as "much more than a mere Man Friday"
- Died 2007, cerebral malaria, Singapore; burial location not documented
- Grimshaw lived alone on the island for his remaining five years

**Books**
- *A Grain of Sand: The Story of One Man and an Island* (Camerapix, January 1996, 210 pages, 94 chapters)
  - Dedication: "For René Antoine Lafortune, much more than a mere Man Friday"
- *Another Grain of Sand: The Story of Two Men and an Island* (2009, 240 pages) — tribute to Lafortune

**Treasure Hunt**
- Discovered two "Unhappily Unknown" pirate graves clearing north-western corner
- Excavated two sites (marked with skull-and-crossbones on current island maps)
- Struck what appeared to be coral concrete at ~20 feet; abandoned excavation
- No documented finds; treated the legend as romantic context, not primary purpose

## 4. LEGAL AND ETHICAL NOTES

Grimshaw died in 2012. He is a public figure. The following applies:

**Safe to use:**
- All confirmed biographical facts (journalism career, island purchase, conservation statistics)
- His public statements (all quotes in Section 1 are from interviews and public sources)
- His named books, their content, and his documented views
- References to the island's documented history, the pirate graves, the national park designation
- Placing the fictional Adam and Brigitte on the island in the past (a visit to a real place that is open to visitors)

**Use with care:**
- Do not invent private conversations, inner thoughts, or private behaviour not documented in any source
- Do not attribute to Grimshaw any specific views on the La Buse cipher, the Bel Ombre entrance, or the underground complex — he has no known connection to these
- The personal recollections in Section 2 can be used as Adam's memories, attributed to Adam — but should not be presented as public historical facts

**The connection between Grimshaw and McAteer:** In the novel, this can be presented as a long-standing friendship between two men who loved the Seychelles — a friendship Adam is aware of and that personal memory validates. McAteer naming Grimshaw in *Hard Times in Paradise* is real and sufficient to establish documented connection. The personal memory layer (McAteer introducing Grimshaw) can be Adam's personal understanding of a connection the books confirm. This is safe.
