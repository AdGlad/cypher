## Mahé Island

The main island. Home to approximately 90% of the population (roughly 80,000 people). The largest island in the Seychelles.

**Geography:**
- Mountainous central range — Morne Seychellois (905m) is the highest point
- Granite geology throughout — the rocks are Precambrian, among the oldest exposed granite on Earth
- Lush tropical vegetation; significant endemic species
- Coastline varies dramatically — some beaches exposed and developed; others remote and inaccessible

**Victoria:**
- Capital city. Smallest capital in the world by some measures (approx. 26,000 population in greater Victoria area)
- The Clock Tower — a small replica of London's Vauxhall Clock Tower, a colonial remnant
- Sir Selwyn Selwyn-Clarke Market — the covered market, Victoria's most visited local attraction; spices, fish, produce, tropical fruit
- National Museum of History — relevant for research on the La Buse mythology and colonial history
- Seychelles National Archives — holds colonial records, land surveys, property documents

**Beau Vallon:**
- Long north-facing beach on northwest Mahé; the most popular and developed beach
- Protected bay; calmer waters than southern beaches
- Tourist infrastructure; but also used by local families
- Clear view to Silhouette Island on the horizon
- Sunsets facing west; spectacular in the dry season

**Bel Ombre:**
- Hill/district area in the northwest of Mahé
- Historically associated with treasure hunting — numerous excavations visible
- Ornamental gardens have been disturbed by centuries of digging
- A somewhat overgrown, layered quality to the landscape — old excavation scars, new vegetation
- Tidal coastline at the base of the Bel Ombre hill — granite formations, complex water access

**Fisherman's Cove:**
- Bel Ombre area, historic waterfront
- A hotel and marina operate here
- The area combines active fishing with tourist infrastructure

## Cerf Island (Île Cerf)

- Small island in the Sainte Anne Marine National Park, just east of Victoria
- Accessible by boat only (approximately 15-minute crossing from Victoria)
- No public vehicles on the island; walking paths only
- A small community of permanent residents
- Marine park surrounds the island — exceptional underwater visibility
- The island has a colonial-era building history; some old stone buildings remain

**For the novel:**
- Juliette's home is here; her family connection roots her to the island
- The boat crossing is a transitional element — going to Cerf Island requires choosing to go
- The island's private quality (no casual tourists, no cars) makes it the right location for McAteer's preserved study

## Praslin Island

- Second-largest island; approximately 30 minutes by ferry from Mahé (or 15 minutes by air)
- More relaxed pace than Mahé; significantly less development
- Home to the Vallée de Mai
- Anse Lazio — one of the world's most beautiful beaches, on the northwest tip
- The main town: Baie Sainte Anne in the south, Grand Anse in the west

**Vallée de Mai:**
- UNESCO World Heritage Site (designated 1983)
- An ancient palm forest dominated by Coco de Mer palms
- Also home to the endemic Black Parrot (Coracopsis barklyi) — Seychelles national bird
- The forest feels prehistoric; the light filters through massive fronds
- Visiting hours strictly managed; overnight access not permitted
- The Seychelles Island Foundation manages the site

## Moyenne Island

- Very small island, approximately 3 hectares
- In the Sainte Anne Marine National Park, near Cerf Island
- Purchased in 1962 by Brendon Grimshaw for a small sum
- Grimshaw lived there alone (with one assistant, René Antoine Lafortune) for decades
- Planted 16,000 trees; established tortoise population; created a bird sanctuary
- Refused all development and buyout offers throughout his life
- Died in 2012; bequeathed the island to the Seychelles nation
- Now a national park; tortoises and birds are its primary feature

## La Digue Island

- Third-largest inhabited island; population approximately 3,000
- Very few motorised vehicles — transport by bicycle and ox-cart
- The most "traditional" feel of the major islands
- Granite formations at the south end are among the most dramatic in the world

**Anse Source d'Argent:**
- On the southwestern shore of La Digue
- Shallow lagoon, massive pink and orange granite boulders, white sand
- Consistently ranked among the world's most photographed beaches
- Accessible through Union Estate (La Digue's main plantation area)
- The boulders provide extraordinary cover and visual complexity — ideal for a scene requiring physical concealment

## Other Relevant Islands

**Silhouette Island:**
- Visible from Beau Vallon; the third-largest Seychelles island
- Largely undeveloped; significant endemic species
- A resort operates on the coast; the interior is protected forest

**North Island:**
- Private island, ultra-luxury resort
- Relevant as Viktor Volkov's type of accommodation preference

## Marine Environment

- The Seychelles sits at the intersection of the Indian Ocean currents
- Coral reefs, granite formations, and extraordinary marine biodiversity
- The Sainte Anne Marine National Park was the first marine park in the Indian Ocean
- Marine conservation is a significant political and economic issue
- OceanVector (Viktor's company) would legitimately operate in this environment — marine data harvesting is a real industry

## Climate Reference

**Two main seasons:**
- **Northwest monsoon (October–April):** Warmer, wetter, calmer seas on the south side; rougher on the north
- **Southeast trade winds (May–September):** Drier, cooler, rougher sea on the north; calmer on the south; the granitic islands don't suffer the cyclones that affect the outer islands

**Novel timing:** The story takes place in the transition between seasons — humidity is building; the air has a quality of anticipation.
