## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Amit is an Israeli boat owner based in the Seychelles, captain of a vessel called *Sea Shell*. He is part of the Gladstone family's long Seychelles history — not a dramatic presence, but a reliable one. The girls grew up taking island trips on his boat during their annual winter holidays. He is the kind of person who becomes part of a family's geography without anyone deciding it: he was simply always there when they needed to get somewhere by water.

In the novel, he is a practical asset and a human anchor — someone who knows the islands from the water, who knows the family across fifteen or more years, and who can get people where they need to go without generating paperwork.

## 3. WHAT IS KNOWN

**[CANON]**

**The boat — *Sea Shell*:**
She is his livelihood and his home in a sense. She operates around Mahé and the inner islands — Cerf, Moyenne, Sainte Anne, Praslin, La Digue. Amit knows these waters the way Edmond knows the land: by accumulated experience over years, through weather and season and tide.

**His relationship with the family:**
He has been taking members of the Gladstone family on island trips for as long as the annual holidays have been running — likely fifteen or more years. He knows Aimee and Chloe from when they were children. He has watched them grow up across their annual visits. He knows Brigitte as a fellow professional who takes the ocean seriously. He knows Adam as someone who came sometimes and stayed on the boat while everyone else swam.

[EDITORIAL]: Adam's water phobia — his refusal to swim in the ocean since the rip current incident — may be something Amit has noticed without understanding it fully. He may have registered that Adam became different around the water at some point; that the man who used to swim now sits on deck. He has not asked.

**What he can do in the novel:**
- Transport between islands — practical, off-the-books, no permit trail
- Local knowledge of the Seychelles from the sea: tidal patterns, channels, what the water looks like at different times of day
- A known face at the marina — he knows who has been coming and going; he will have noticed Viktor's operation
- The approach to Bel Ombre: the tidal channel entrance is accessible from the sea as well as land; Amit's knowledge of the coastal water at Bel Ombre may be directly relevant

## 4. EDITORIAL SUGGESTIONS
*[NON-CANONICAL UNTIL AUTHOR CONFIRMS]*

**[EDITORIAL 1 — His significance for Act 3]**
The tidal channel at Bel Ombre is most naturally approached by sea — arriving by boat at low tide, reading the water. Amit knows these waters. He may be the one who positions *Sea Shell* at the right moment for the right approach. He would do this because the family asked him to, not because he fully understands what they are doing.

**[EDITORIAL 2 — Adam and Amit]**
Adam dislikes dogs and loves cats. He has mild social anxiety and prefers one-to-one conversation over groups. A boat is an excellent environment for Adam — contained, purposeful, with a natural excuse to look at the horizon rather than at the room. His best conversations in the Seychelles may happen on *Sea Shell*. Amit, as a man who spends his days in a practical relationship with the physical world, is likely someone Adam can talk to without social calculation.

**[EDITORIAL 3 — What Amit has seen]**
Viktor's survey team will have chartered boats, hired local captains, run equipment offshore. Amit, operating at the marina and on the water, will have seen the activity without understanding its purpose. He may have a piece of information about Viktor's operation — a vessel he noticed, a route he saw repeated — that he offers to Adam casually, not knowing it matters.

**[EDITORIAL 4 — His Seychellois life]**
An Israeli living in the Seychelles has made a specific set of choices. He came from somewhere and chose this. The Seychelles has a small but established international community of people who arrived meaning to pass through and stayed. Amit's reasons for being here — whatever they are — should feel personal rather than generic. He is not a "local colour" character. He is a man with a history who chose this particular sea.

**[EDITORIAL 5 — Aimee and Amit]**
Aimee grew up on this boat. She knows *Sea Shell* the way she knows the Glacis house — through the body, not through description. She knows where things are kept, what the engine sounds like, how the deck moves in a swell. On *Sea Shell*, she is at home in a way that is different from her usual self. Amit sees her as she is rather than as who she is becoming. This could be a useful scene — someone who knew her as a child, seeing her now, in the place she has always been most herself.
