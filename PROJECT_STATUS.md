## Author Decisions — RESOLVED

1. **Prologue:** Two-scene prologue. Scene 1 — La Buse at the entrance, c.1727 (Bel Ombre, before dawn). Scene 2 — capture and gallows, 7 July 1730 (Saint-Denis, Réunion; cipher thrown into crowd). Ends: *Two hundred and ninety-nine years later.* Reader doesn't understand Scene 1 until the end.
2. **Violence:** No deaths. Family thriller. Dax is operational only — no physical threat.
3. **La Buse cipher:** Use the real La Roncière 1934 cryptogram. In-world genuine; scholarly uncertainty is ZARYA's blind spot.
4. **Viktor's AI:** Named ZARYA (Russian for "dawn" — ISS module resonance). Replaces AURORA throughout.
5. **Archive creator:** Abel Deschamps — French Huguenot scholar who fled France after the Revocation of the Edict of Nantes (October 1685). Arrived in the Indian Ocean region late 1680s–1690s. Died on Mahé; archive sealed in the complex.
6. **Brigitte:** Based in Australia. Present throughout the novel in the Seychelles. Half-Seychellois. Perfect French. Full character, not phone voice.
7. **Family backstory:** When Aimee was 6 and Chloe was 7.5, the family spent a year in the Seychelles — GFC stripped Adam's funding; Juliette diagnosed with cancer. Girls attended local school. This is where the Creole rhyme was learned.
8. **Chloe:** New character — Aimee's older sister, 18 months older, present throughout. Also has Creole, also knows the rhyme — but cannot perform the sequence. She is the novel's clearest demonstration of knowing vs. having.
9. **Aimee:** Age 24 in 2026. Based in Australia. University of Western Australia (deferred).
10. **Adam:** University of Western Australia. British-Australian.
11. **SYBIL:** Kind, empathetic AI. Errs toward compassion. Notices the human alongside the analytical. Built-in uncertainty-flagging. Contrast with ZARYA (confident, cold, no shadows).
12. **Series:** Trilogy confirmed. "Who built the complex" held for Book 2. Dax open ending deliberate.
13. **Institute name:** The Deschamps-McAteer Institute for Natural History and Conservation.
14. **The Creole rhyme:** *Ti chat ki pe dòmi* — three verses, physical sequence embedded in Verse 2, five steps. Key line: *"Laport louvri pour seki kone"* ("The door opens for those who know"). Full text in `notes/creole-rhyme.md`. Requires cultural consultant review before publication.
15. **Archive — first impression:** Stillness. Sealed air. Objects undisturbed for three centuries. The overwhelming sense: the scholar organised this for a reader, not for storage. It is a library, not a vault. First action: Adam says nothing. Aimee touches the granite shelf.
16. **Viktor and Mikhail:** Mikhail (Viktor's partner, died 1998) surfaces in Chapter 22 — Viktor alone on the terrace above Victoria, facing his first real failure. He built Proxima for Mikhail; ZARYA is the best thing he has built. ZARYA cannot solve this. The archive would have been proof that what he built is sufficient.
17. **Adam sees Aimee (the turn):** Chapter 25. After *"I know what it is,"* Adam stops himself mid-question, recognising he was about to ask the wrong thing. He asks instead: *"Tell me what you mean."* He listens without routing her answer through SYBIL. He says: *"All right."* That is the turn.
