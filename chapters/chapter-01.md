# Chapter 1 — The Package

**Act:** Act 1
**POV:** Adam Gladstone
**Location:** University of Perth — Adam's office
**Timeline Day:** Day 1

---

## Chapter Purpose

Deliver the inciting object. Introduce Adam's character through his reaction. Establish the novel's opening question: why did a dead historian send him this book?

---

## Opening Beat

Adam at his desk, late afternoon. He is working with SYBIL on an unrelated research problem. The package from the Seychelles is on the corner of his desk — he has been not-opening it for approximately forty minutes. This tells us everything about him.

---

## Key Scenes

### Scene 1 — The Office
**Location:** Perth University, mathematics department
**Characters present:** Adam (alone)
**What happens:** Adam finally opens the parcel. Inside: McAteer's annotated copy of *Rivals in Eden*. He reads the annotations with analytical attention. Then the handwritten note, inside the back cover.
**What it means:** The inciting note — "The entrance is in the books. All of them." — is delivered. The mystery begins.

### Scene 2 — SYBIL's First Appearance
**Location:** Same
**Characters present:** Adam, SYBIL
**What happens:** Adam photographs the note and the annotations and runs them through SYBIL. SYBIL identifies the handwriting as consistent with the signed annotations (McAteer's hand). She flags the phrase "the entrance" as ambiguous — entrance to what? She cannot determine.
**What it means:** SYBIL is introduced as a tool that works well within its domain and reaches its limit immediately on the first significant question.

---

## Clue / Information Delivered

- McAteer's annotated *Rivals in Eden* exists
- The note: "The entrance is in the books. All of them."
- Juliette's name and Cerf Island address on the parcel

---

## Character Work

Adam's forty minutes of not-opening the parcel tells us he processes anticipation analytically — he is preparing himself, not procrastinating. His emotional response to the note is felt before it is acknowledged. He reads it twice. He doesn't call anyone immediately. He thinks.

---

## Chapter Ending Hook

Adam opens McAteer's book to a page that McAteer has circled heavily. A specific paragraph about the Bel Ombre area. The language is oddly precise for a general history. He reads it three times and doesn't understand why it feels important. He reaches for the phone.
