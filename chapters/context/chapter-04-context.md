## What Happens — Beat by Beat

**Beat 1 — The morning before departure**
Adam has booked his flight. He is in his office for one working day before he leaves. Three notebooks: one for McAteer's annotations, one for SYBIL's preliminary output, one blank — reserved for what he doesn't know yet. He leaves blanks deliberately. This is methodological.

He is packing research files, not clothes. The priority of things.

**Beat 2 — SYBIL delivers the full analysis**
SYBIL has had all five books for 36 hours. Her full output:

```
[SYBIL: McAteer corpus analysis complete. Confidence: 83%.
Three anomaly categories identified:

1. GEOGRAPHICAL PRECISION CLUSTERS
   Fourteen passages across Books 1, 2, and 4 carry specificity
   of location reference inconsistent with surrounding narrative
   register. Seven of fourteen cluster within a 200-word radius
   of the phrase "Bel Ombre" or proximate terms. Statistical
   improbability of random distribution: p < 0.02.

2. FOOTNOTE CITATION PATTERN
   Books 1, 2, and 4 share a footnote citation structure that
   departs from McAteer's standard methodology. Citations in the
   anomalous passages reference primary sources not confirmed
   in McAteer's declared bibliography. This may indicate working
   notes not published, or sources acquired independently.

3. NEAR-DUPLICATE PHRASES (cross-book)
   Two passages — Book 2, p.147 and Book 4, p.203 — share
   phrasing that is near-identical but not identical, and neither
   quotes from a common identified source. The divergence between
   them is precise: three words changed; one sentence reordered.
   This degree of similarity across publications separated by
   19 years suggests working from the same underlying note.

   I do not have sufficient context to interpret the significance
   of these anomalies. They may be the product of McAteer's
   research methodology. They may be something else.
   I notice you are leaving for the Seychelles tomorrow.]
```

Adam reads this output three times. He photographs it. He opens the third notebook.

**Beat 3 — What this means (or might mean)**
He works through the logic carefully. SYBIL has found a pattern. The pattern is real — p < 0.02 is not coincidence. But a pattern is not an argument. Three things are possible:
1. McAteer's research methodology naturally produced these clusters — dense archival work on the same primary site will produce location precision regardless of intention
2. McAteer noticed the pattern and began, over the course of five books, consciously reinforcing it — not planting clues but documenting what he suspected
3. The pattern points to something that was always there, independent of McAteer's awareness of it

McAteer's note — *"I didn't put it there. It was already there"* — argues for option 3. But option 3 would require that the historical record of Bel Ombre is more structured than anyone has noticed. Adam is not yet ready to believe this. He is keeping all three options.

**Beat 4 — What SYBIL cannot do**
Adam asks SYBIL the next question:

*"What is the underlying structure? What are the fourteen passages pointing toward?"*

```
[SYBIL: Insufficient data for structural interpretation.
The passages reference topographical features, tidal conditions,
and land ownership records. A unified structural reading would
require knowledge of the physical site that I do not have access
to remotely. On-site correlation is the appropriate next step.
I can prepare a comparative location reference map for the
fourteen passages if that would assist.]
```

This is the limit. She can find the pattern. She cannot say what it means without being there.

Adam: *"Yes. Prepare the map."*

She does. He prints it.

**Beat 5 — The note**
Adam re-reads McAteer's handwritten note one more time before packing the annotated book:

*"The entrance is in the books. All of them. I didn't put it there. It was already there."*

He has been treating this as a hypothesis. He is beginning to treat it as an instruction.

*Cut.*

## Adam in This Chapter

- This is Adam at his most methodological — the pleasure he takes in the pattern is real and professional; this is the kind of problem he is built for
- The three notebooks are a detail that reveals character: he reserves a blank one before he knows what he'll need it for. This is how he thinks.

## SYBIL in This Chapter

*Full capability chapter — the most SYBIL does in the novel.*

- Her full anomaly analysis is her most complex output in Book 1
- She structures her finding in three numbered categories — this is her natural form; precise, ordered, transparent
- The confidence level (83%) is lower than her handwriting match from Chapter 1 — she is more uncertain about interpretation than identification, and she says so
- Her aside ("I notice you are leaving for the Seychelles tomorrow") is her third act of noticing — she knows Adam's calendar, she notes the convergence
- She offers to prepare the location map without being asked — anticipating what would be useful. This is her at her best: extending his thinking rather than replacing it.
- She flags what she cannot do with the same directness she brings to what she can: *"On-site correlation is the appropriate next step."* She knows when the job exceeds her tools.

## The Three Anomalies — Technical Notes

These need to feel specific enough to be plausible as the product of computational analysis. Keep the passage-reference numbers consistent with the actual McAteer books if possible (Books 1, 2, 4 = *Rivals in Eden*, *Hard Times in Paradise*, *To Be a Nation*):

- **Geographical precision clusters:** The fourteen passages will be detailed in Chapter 21 (when Adam solves the entrance); here they are a finding without full interpretation
- **Footnote citation pattern:** The anomalous footnotes reference sources McAteer had but didn't publish — probably field notes from his later research trips that he chose not to include in the formal bibliography
- **Near-duplicate phrases:** The shared language across two books 19 years apart is the most arresting anomaly — it implies McAteer returned to the same underlying note or observation repeatedly
