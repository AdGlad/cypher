# Prologue — THE ENTRANCE
*Mahé, Seychelles — c.1727*

---

**Status:** Outlined. Context file ready. Not yet drafted.

**Word target:** 500–800 words

**Function:** Plant the image the reader will not understand until Chapter 30. La Buse at the entrance to the complex — alone, efficient, unromanticised. He retrieves what he came for. He looks at the archive and does not understand it. He scratches a marker into the granite wall. He leaves before the tide returns.

**Context file:** `chapters/context/prologue-context.md`

**Drafting notes:**
- Close third person in La Buse's POV — the only scene from his perspective in the novel
- No dialogue
- Past tense throughout
- Spare prose; no romanticism
- The scratch on the wall is the detail that will echo in Chapter 30
- The archive is present but unexplained — do not name the creator; do not describe beyond the lantern's reach
- Ends on: *Two hundred and ninety-nine years later.*
